My listing client took advantage of Compass Concierge to re-position an Upper East Side co-op in estate-like condition and achieved close to full asking price after only 35 days — less than one-quarter the average number of days on market!
 
Challenge:
Sell a spacious but dated one-bedroom co-op in poor condition in a competitive submarket with abundant inventory. Situated in a 1960s building on a prime East 70s block, the apartment was in a neglected state with an original kitchen and bath. However, the unit had great attributes too, including a large, flexible layout, open views and great light.

The owner knew that to market and sell the apartment, he would have to make an upfront investment in improvements. He didn't know how to proceed, nor did he have the time or resources to manage an entire apartment makeover. However, he viewed me as a trusted adviser and reached out to me directly to sell his home.
 
Solution:
I presented a strong argument for undertaking the necessary cosmetic upgrades and for staging the apartment in preparation for sale. For nearly all homes in a competitive market, improvements and staging are necessary to generate buyer traffic and increase the odds for a successful sale. 

As Compass Chief Evangelist Leonard Steinberg points out, sellers used to need Patience and a good Price to sell their homes. Today, the third P is Perfection. Presenting a beautiful image for your home is critical when buyers have many alternatives.
 
Results:
The striking listing image of the well-appointed living/dining area was so powerful that it drove tremendous interest and buyer traffic to the home. The improvements and staging only emphasized all of the positive attributes of the apartment, while removing focus from the original kitchen and bath.

The apartment was priced to sell at $850,000, and we felt strongly that the owner would easily get back his upfront investment of $32,000. In fact, we had an accepted offer after only two weeks and a signed contract after only 35 days on market. The apartment sold just slightly below the asking price.

Without the updates and staging, we estimated an asking price under $800,000. By tapping into the resources of Compass Concierge, the seller created value and, based on the agreed upon sale price of $845,000, he more than recouped his upfront investment of $32,000 with a rather quick sale relative to market.
